Population in Asia (2020)

Recording a total population of over 4.6 billion people with the 3rd highest growth rate of 0.83%, Asia is Earth’s largest and most populous continent, making up nearly 60% of the world’s population.

Population breakdown

Excluding Asia’s two most populated nations, Indonesia and Pakistan have the highest populations in Asia. Interestingly, the cumulative world population share of these countries in the top 10 is nearly 5% lower than China’s world population share alone.

Variation in population

Fertility rates and migration are two key factors that influence change in a country’s population. In 2020, Bahrain had the highest increase in migrants while Syria had the highest decrease. Looking at the scatterplot, there is a negative correlation between fertility rates and median age. Majority of median ages lie within 25-35 years with fertility rates between 2-3 births per woman.

The countries with the greatest population increase include nations such as Bahrain, Oman, and Syria. As mentioned above, one of the main factors that play a key role is fertility rate. From the scatterplot representing the median age and fertility rate correlation of the 15 highest growth nations, a higher average fertility rate and lower median age can be observed.
